useful information:


	- it is possible to use multiple times the same subnet in a network, for example:

	  +----------------+----------------+
	  |IP		   |Subnet Mask	    |
	  +----------------+----------------+
	  |192.168.100.1   |255.255.255.252 |
	  +---------------------------------+
 	  |192.168.100.2   |255.255.255.252 |
	  +---------------------------------+
	  |192.168.100.253 |255.255.255.252 |
	  +---------------------------------+
	  |192.168.100.254 |255.255.255.252 |
	  +---------------------------------+
	  |192.168.100.5   |255.255.255.252 |
 	  +---------------------------------+
	  |192.168.100.6   |255.255.255.252 |
	  +---------------------------------+
	 
	  in this case all machines will be able to communicate.
	  the subnet 255.255.255.252 only allows 4 channels:
	 
	  0
	  4
	  8
	  12
	  ...

	  you just have to make sure they are all in different blocks. 
	  (revelant for level 7)

	- ips to avoid using
	  +------------------------------+------------------+
	  | Range                        | CIDR             |
	  +------------------------------+------------------+
	  | 10.0.0.0 – 10.255.255.255    | 10.0.0.0/8       |
	  +------------------------------+------------------+
	  | 172.16.0.0 – 172.31.255.255  | 172.16.0.0/12    |
	  +------------------------------+------------------+
	  | 192.168.0.0 – 192.168.255.255| 192.168.0.0/16   |
	  +------------------------------+------------------+
